# Special Needs Support
Overview
KEEL Special Needs Support provides trained professionals who assist children with developmental or learning differences both in school and at home. These professionals help create structured environments that support learning, communication, and independence.

What Special Support Professionals Do
- Implement structured behavioural strategies
- Support learning and academic engagement
- Help children build social and emotional skills
- Assist with routine building and organisation
- Provide consistent guidance alongside parents and educators

Experience
Professionals in this category may include:
- psychology graduates
- special education trainees
- experienced support workers

Training
KEEL provides training in:
- developmental and learning differences
- behaviour management techniques
- communication and engagement strategies

Assessment
Every placement begins with an evaluation of the child’s needs to ensure the right professional match.
