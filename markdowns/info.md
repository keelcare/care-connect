1. Child Care (Developmental Nannies)
Overview
KEEL Child Care professionals provide reliable home-based care for young children while supporting their developmental needs. Our caregivers focus on safety, routine, and engaging activities that help children build confidence and independence.
What Our Child Care Professionals Do
	•	Supervise and ensure the child’s safety and well-being 
	•	Support daily routines such as meals, playtime, and rest 
	•	Engage children in learning-based play and activities 
	•	Help build communication and social interaction skills 
	•	Encourage developmental milestones through structured play 
Training
All KEEL caregivers complete a basic training program covering:
	•	child development basics 
	•	safety and supervision 
	•	positive behaviour guidance 
	•	communication with young children 
Verification
Every caregiver is:
	•	identity verified 
	•	background checked 
	•	trained in child safety protocols 
Availability
Caregivers are matched based on location proximity and commute independently to the family’s home.

2. Shadow Teacher
Overview
KEEL Shadow Teachers provide one-on-one classroom support for students who benefit from additional attention in school. They work alongside teachers to help children stay engaged, follow classroom instructions, and participate confidently in learning activities.
What Shadow Teachers Do
	•	Support students in staying focused during lessons 
	•	Help reinforce teacher instructions 
	•	Assist with classroom routines and transitions 
	•	Encourage social interaction with peers 
	•	Provide gentle behavioural guidance when required 
Many students supported by shadow teachers may have attention or learning differences such as
Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder or
Autism Spectrum Disorder.
Training
KEEL shadow teachers undergo training in:
	•	classroom behaviour support 
	•	understanding ADHD and autism 
	•	communication and engagement strategies 
	•	child safety practices 
Placement
Shadow teachers are matched based on:
	•	the child’s needs 
	•	school environment 
	•	proximity to the school location. 

3. Special Needs Support
Overview
KEEL Special Needs Support provides trained professionals who assist children with developmental or learning differences both in school and at home. These professionals help create structured environments that support learning, communication, and independence.
What Special Support Professionals Do
	•	Implement structured behavioural strategies 
	•	Support learning and academic engagement 
	•	Help children build social and emotional skills 
	•	Assist with routine building and organisation 
	•	Provide consistent guidance alongside parents and educators 
Experience
Professionals in this category may include:
	•	psychology graduates 
	•	special education trainees 
	•	experienced support workers 
Training
KEEL provides training in:
	•	developmental and learning differences 
	•	behaviour management techniques 
	•	communication and engagement strategies 
Assessment
Every placement begins with an evaluation of the child’s needs to ensure the right professional match.
