# Shadow Teacher
Overview
KEEL Shadow Teachers provide one-on-one classroom support for students who benefit from additional attention in school. They work alongside teachers to help children stay engaged, follow classroom instructions, and participate confidently in learning activities.

What Shadow Teachers Do
- Support students in staying focused during lessons
- Help reinforce teacher instructions
- Assist with classroom routines and transitions
- Encourage social interaction with peers
- Provide gentle behavioural guidance when required

Many students supported by shadow teachers may have attention or learning differences such as Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder or Autism Spectrum Disorder.

Training
KEEL shadow teachers undergo training in:
- classroom behaviour support
- understanding ADHD and autism
- communication and engagement strategies
- child safety practices

Placement
Shadow teachers are matched based on:
- the child’s needs
- school environment
- proximity to the school location.
